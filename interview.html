David Fan is my interviewee who is currently 50, born in Taipei, Taiwan. He immigrated in 1995 at the age of 22 and is currently residing in Arcadia, California with his family.

1. Who did you leave behind? Why and how did you feel? Did they support you?

I want to find out if their relations from their home country supported them in any way and if they felt like they made the right decision.

Response: I left some of my relatives and friends in Taiwan and felt like it was the right decision as America brought in a new experience for me to see. My parents supported me. They are also in America and they immigrated before I did.

2. How was the process of immigrating to America? How long did it take? Was it challenging?

I want to know their experience through the whole immigration process because it is important to understand the struggles they went through to get here like Ellis island.

Response: If I remember, the process was pretty easy since my parents already immigrated here. No, it was not challenging.

3. How did your culture differ from the culture in America? Did you assimilate into American culture?

I chose this question because my interviewee may have a different perspective on American culture whether they found any differences between the two cultures.

Response: The culture in Taiwan is different in America because over in Taiwan, people are Buddhism and they speak Mandarin as their language. They are also very friendly and Taiwan has one of the wealthiest economy probably in Asia. It was pretty crowded too since Taiwan’s a lot smaller than America. Yes, I did adopt American culture. I enjoy the freedom a lot more because US made me feel more “free”. My mother and father also live here so it was fine to adopt American cultures they like it.

4. Did any American values or culture feel interesting to you or did you feel detached from it?

Similar to question #3, I chose this question because they may agreements or disagreements about certain American values/culture that may affect their life. Maybe they felt accepted or neglected by America.

Response: I think American values/culture felt interesting to me. I experience the different holidays America has. I tried different cultures of food here and I really liked it. I could also meet new people that are not only Taiwanese and they were really friendly.

5. Do you identify yourself as a patriotic or nationalist in America?

I chose this question because America is currently #1 in the World power ranking and maybe this will affect their perspective for their country and other countries. If they feel like they’re loyal to America and don’t care for other countries or they’re superior to other countries.

Response: I don't think I'm either one. I love both Taiwan and America and support both equally.

6. Do you find living in America or in your own country a lot more preferable? Why?

I want to figure out if their decision in immigrating to America made their life enjoyable or if their own country is more suitable for them.

Response: I prefer to live in America because I enjoy the beauty and nature compared to Taiwan because in Taiwan, it’s rainier and foggier. I can enjoy working on my garden here because the weather is nice so my plants don't get overwatered.

7. Are you relatively interested in politics? If so, What is your opinion on our current system in America compared to Taiwan?

I chose this question because many people have their own political opinion on the current situations/affairs going on around the world like how people disagree with China’s government system/ccp.

Response: Yes, politics, Iam pretty interested in politics in both Taiwan and America. I watch the news sometimes to stay updated and I also voted this year. My opinion is that the American government is better. I think because I have more freedom and rights compared to Taiwan but I think Taiwan’s government is still good enough.

8. Have you ever experienced any discrimination based on your nationality, race, beliefs, and political opinion?

This question is important because many immigrants have been suppressed because of their nationality and I want to know if they have in any way experienced any disruptions in America.

Response: I have seen some problems on our nationality like this year because of the coronavirus on the news a lot. To be honest, I hardly experience any discrimination because I live around a safe city and Arcadia have a lot of asains just like me but I have experienced discrimination for my belief in buddhism because I think America is mostly Christian.

9. Have you ever regret your decision in immigrating to America?

This question is important because America is known as a country prospering globally, providing benefits and opportunities to many citizens. I want to know if they regret their decision in coming here or they did have a successful transition here.

Response: No, I am satisfied in America and I think America is better than Taiwan as the weather and environment is much better and I feel more space because it’s less busy/crowded here.

10. Have you ever considered yourself a citizen of the United States?

I chose this question because I wanted to know if they identify themselves as an “American” who Americanized. If they have adapted to the current society.

Response: Yes, I consider myself a citizen of the United States. I really enjoy the country and environment. I learned english and learning english was a fun experience because learning a new language is always hard. I feel like I have Americanized into the current society.
